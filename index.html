Another dating site? There's so many out there already! There's so many sites that claim to bring people together by various metrics or some by almost none whatsoever. So what would make probability dating different? Well, the big question a person has before sending the first message is "Will he/she even be interested?" and on the flip side "Why are all these people I'm not interested in messaging me?" And that's where we come in.

No one can say for sure whether or not someone's a good match, but our users will put in honest estimations of their physical attributes (height, body type, ethnicity, etc), and other factors such as religion (and how religious they are). They will then rank factors on a scale on ("deal breaker", "dislike", "neutral", "like"). When you look at someone's profile you will see the probability of that person being interested in you.

FAQ

What factors come into play?
Physical, activity types, activity level, belief systems are the general categories we look at.
What does "physical factors" involve?
Height, body type, ethnicity mainly. Other factors will fall into this category such as hair, body hair, tattoos, hygiene.
How do these "rating levels" work?
Basically if your type is something, you would answer "like" to the question. That means if your ideal guy is very muscular, you would put "like" for that. If the thing in question you are ok with but it's not exactly your "type" you put "neutral". For example, if you like slim women, but you're perfectly ok with an average shape woman you put "neutral". "Dislike" is for things that you don't actually like but it's not a deal breaker. For example, if you prefer your women to be shorter than you, but you'd be ok with a woman taller than you as long as she was great otherwise you would put "dislike". Finally if something is out of the question for you, you put "deal breaker". That means for example if you absolutely refuse to date someone who is highly religious no matter how perfect he or she is in every other factor, then rate that person as a "deal breaker". If a person has a trait that is a deal breaker for you his or her probability of a match with you will automatically be zero.
How do the ratings work?
The ratings will be broken down into categories: physical match. Activity match (how likely you'll get along doing activities together). Belief system match (how in sync your views of the world are). You will also have an overall match. If you have any "deal breaker" traits, it will be just shown as no match though. Each category otherwise will be ranked as "poor" (very poor, poor), "neutral" (slighty poor, slightly good), "good" (good, very good).
What if you lie about yourself?
There's nothing we can do to stop this from happening. If another user reports you as a liar, we might ask for verification from both parties. We can't just accept erroneous reports because people may be reported out of spite.
Why don't you judge on interests?
People can have very different interests and be very compatible. Incompatibility comes more from whether or not you are a big active person and your partner refuses to do active activities. Or if one person is a risktaker and the other is extremely risk adverse.
